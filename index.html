My name is Alexander Golin.

     I'm a senior at Tufts University in Somerville, MA, and I'm pursuing a dual degree in Human Factors Engineering and Computer Science. My greatest passion in life is music, and there is nothing I love more than finding new ways to combine psyschological and social principals with cutting edge technology to create unique solutions in the field of music.

     I moved to New York City when I was three years old, and have lived all over the borough of Manhattan; from Chelsea and Hell's Kitchen to Washington Heights and East Harlem. I attended Hunter College Campus Schools from Kindergarten through Senior year of high school.

     When I got to Tufts, I initally declared my major as Mechanical Engineering, but I later realized my passion lies far more in finding the hybrid paths forged by the interactions between psychology and engineering. I redeclared as a Human Factors Engineering and Computer Science double major, taking a particular interest in user centric web design. My courseload has included classes ranging anywhere between engineering sciences, applied computer programming, theoretical computer science, psychology and psychological methods, and design. My younger brother Nicholas, my favorite person in the world, joined me at Tufts as a member of the graduating class of 2019.

     I am a member of Tau Beta Pi, the Engineering Honors Society, and I am the Webmaster of the Tufts chapther of the Human Factors and Ergonomics Society, which has a new website in the works. In addition to my academic involvement, I am also Music Director of 91.5 WMFO Medford, Tufts Freeform Radio, and I am a leader in the Tufts DIY Concert Booking Collective called Midnight/Applejam.
